Canha Medieval Fair 2025

11-13 July 2025

For three days, Canha, known for its tranquility and natural beauty, was transformed into a true medieval setting.

The oldest town in the municipality of Montijo held a historical reenactment transporting visitors back to 1235, the year in which Vila Nova de Canha received its charter from the Order of Santiago during the reign of King Sancho II.

Moonlight Suppers

With music, historical reenactments, and plenty of entertainment between courses, “From the Lands of Canha to the Table”, medieval dinners from the time of King Sancho II attracted large crowds.

Tournaments

Weapons and horseback tournaments and parades alluding to the era completed the immersion in the Middle Ages.

Dances

There were games for children and adults, traditional and Arabic dances, horsemen, theater, an exhibition of the royal chambers, and a colloquium on Canha in the Middle Ages, facade contests, immersive games, and a whole magical atmosphere of the time with its craftsmen and merchants.

With free admission, this Fair was mainly held within the gates of the village of Canha, in the square and in the Canha Ethnographic Museum, and counted on the indispensable participation of all the groups and associations of Canha: Sta. Casa da Misericórdia, Associação dos Bombeiros Voluntários de Canha, Casa do Povo de Canha, Comissão de Festas, as well as many other groups organized with the people of Canha specifically for this event.

What set the Medieval Fair apart was the quality of the participating groups and the interaction with the local people, who also took part in small theatrical performances and traditional dances, which, thanks to CIRCULODEMAJALIS, made the whole festival the success that it was.

This event was promoted by the Montijo City Council and organized by our association CIRCULODEMAJALIS with the support of the Canha Parish Council and Turismo de Portugal through Dr. Carla Salsinha, president of the Lisbon Region Regional Tourism Authority.
